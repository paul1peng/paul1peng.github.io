Paul Peng

Artist Biography & Statement

Biography

Paul Peng (b. 1994) is a drawing-based contemporary artist whose work combines non-representational mark-making with cartoon figuration. He holds a BCSA in Computer Science and Art from Carnegie Mellon University’s Intercollege Degree Program and has also studied classical drawing at Barnstone Studios in Coplay, PA and experimental drawing at the Ox-Bow School of Art in Saugatuck, MI.

Since completing undergrad in 2017, Paul has shown in multiple group and solo exhibitions across Pittsburgh and was also a member of the eleventh cohort of the Brew House Association’s Distillery Emerging Artist Residency program. He has also collaborated with experimental comics publisher 2dcloud, who has published excerpts from his sketchbook on their website and released an interview with him in their fifth issue of Altcomics Magazine.

Alongside his drawing and comics practice, Paul has experimented in the past with stone lithography and conceptual writing. He currently lives and works from Pittsburgh, PA.

Statement

Working from my personal history and experience with furry culture and mass media fandoms, I approach cartooning as a form of abstract expressionist drawing. My work weaves soft boyish faces, monstrous fantasy bodies, and lushly rendered urban and pastoral settings through a warp of stormy non-representational marks, creating dense pictures that contest and complicate narrative meaning-making in favor of the raw, open-ended energy of an atomic cartoon moment.

I want to explore what cartoon figures and spaces born from queer teen alienation can express in a world where that alienation used to exist but no longer does, and how these expressions can carve out new ways of imaging and imagining how gay bodies navigate this larger world.
